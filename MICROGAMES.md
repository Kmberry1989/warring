# Microgames

All microgames are touch-first, short, and intended to work the same in solo and versus.

## Original Set

- `fly`: tap the moving fly before time runs out.
- `dont`: avoid touching the screen for the full timer.
- `pop`: pop every balloon before they escape.
- `catch`: move the basket and catch enough falling bread.
- `dodge`: move the runner away from falling hazards.
- `swipe`: swipe in the shown direction.
- `triple`: tap the center button exactly three times quickly.
- `hold`: press and hold until the release cue.
- `mash`: tap repeatedly to reach the target count.
- `trace`: rub out the circle path fast enough.
- `match`: reveal and pair all matching icons.
- `avoid`: tap every green target without hitting red ones.
- `pump`: inflate the balloon to the target size.
- `whack`: hit enough popping targets before they disappear.
- `stop`: stop the moving marker inside the green zone.
- `drag`: drag the marker into the target.
- `scratch`: scratch away the cover to reveal the prize.
- `find`: spot the odd emoji in the grid.
- `feed`: drag the food into the mouth.
- `order`: tap `1`, then `2`, then `3` in order.
- `jump`: jump over the incoming obstacle.
- `slice`: drag across the melon to cut it.
- `scrub`: wipe away all dirt blobs.
- `math`: solve the quick addition prompt.
- `shoot`: tap the moving target without missing.
- `simon`: watch the flash pattern, then repeat it. The UI now shows `WATCH`, `REPEAT`, and per-tap progress.
- `rhythm`: tap when the shrinking ring reaches the sweet spot.
- `mug`: stop the moving mug inside the grab zone.
- `spy`: keep the spotlight over the sneaking target.

## Added Set

- `stack`: drop moving blocks and keep enough overlap to build a 4-block tower.
- `balance`: keep the cursor inside the drifting green zone for 2 seconds.
- `sort`: drag each item into the matching bin.
- `zap`: start on blue, draw to yellow, and avoid the red blockers.
- `bounce`: keep the ball alive by tapping rhythmic boosts.
- `decoy`: tap the real star instead of the decoys.

## Design Rules

- Time-to-understand should be under one second.
- Time-to-finish should usually be under five seconds.
- Input should be obvious from the screen state, not only from the prompt word.
- A loss should feel attributable to player action, not hidden rules.
